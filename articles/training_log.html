  • Austin Half Marathon (02/20/22): 13.1 mile run
    • Chip time: 1:51:48, 1311st out of 7315 overall (top 18%)
    • Average pace and HR: 8:32/mi, average 183 BPM
    Race report: My first time racing a half marathon! I'm going to start writing separate blog posts for race reports, and you can read this one here. But in short, the first 10k felt jolly, I started hurting around mile 8, and had to push through grueling hills to a sprint finish (now I can't walk up stairs).

  • Texas Tough Duathlon (11/14/21): 5k run, 16mi bike, 2.5k run
    • Overall time: 1:33:19, Collegiate M15-19 3rd out of 11 (podium!), 19th out of 62 overall (top 30%)
    • Run split: 25:05, 8:08/mi, 25th overall (top 40%)
    • Bike split: 55:17, 17.26 mph, 11th overall (top 18%)
    • Run split: 11:18, 7:20/mi, 20th overall (top 32%)
    • Transitions: 0:45 (T1), 5th overall (top 16%),
      • 0:58 (T2), 25th overall (top 40%)
    Race report: This was my first race with the UT triathlon team- it was a ton of fun! I took the first hilly 5k out really slow then picked it up on the descent (6:52 mile split!). For the ride my goal was to stay in the aero bars as long as possible; I had some nice back and forths with an A&M girl with a disk wheel. Also those hills were brutal... The final run was extremely painful. Ryan (a teammate) was trailing behind and told me "Don't let me pass you!", which gave me the motivation to finish strong.

  • Blackland Triathlon (9/06/21): 300m swim, 13.8m bike, 5k run
    • Overall time: 1:18:26, AG M18-24 2nd out of 10 (podium!), 43rd out of 204 overall (top 22%)
    • Swim split: 5:17, 1:46/HM, 22nd overall (top 11%)
    • Bike split: 43:57, 18.8 mph, 69th overall (top 34%)
    • Run split: 25:44, 8:15/mi, 52nd overall (top 26%)
    • Transitions: 2:20 (T1), 32nd overall (top 16%),
      • 1:06 (T2), 28th overall (top 14%)
    Race report: My first triathlon! This race took place at my home pool (Oak Point, where I lifeguard) and I raced it with one of my head guards. Definitely caught the triathlon bug after this one! I came in with low expectations and blew them out of the water (especially on the bike leg), passing people with road bikes on my slow hybrid. The feeling of standing on the podium (even if only 10 people were in your AG) is amazing. (I doubt this will happen next race though.) After this race I "officially" joined the Texas Triathlon team. Hook' em!
